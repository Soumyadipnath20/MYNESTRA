Types of Menstrual Flow and Period Blood Color

What is a Menstrual Cycle?

Each month, the body undergoes hormone-driven changes to prepare for a possible pregnancy. This series of events is known as the menstrual cycle.

The ovarian cycle controls the production and release of eggs and the cyclic release of estrogen and progesterone. The uterine cycle governs the preparation and maintenance of the lining of the uterus to receive an embryo.

These cycles are concurrent and coordinated, normally lasting between 21 and 35 days, with a median of 28 days.

Light Flow

Occurs at the beginning or end of the cycle. Requires minimal absorbency protection.

Moderate Flow

Common in the middle days of the period. Pads, tampons, or cups provide effective protection.

Heavy Flow

Leads to frequent pad/tampon changes and can last several days.
